What is AIDS?

'Human immunodeficiency virus (HIVS)' is the virus that causes 'Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome (AIDS)'. When a person becomes infected with HIV, the virus attacks and weakens the immune system. As the immune system weakens, the person is at risk for getting life-threatening infections and cancers.


How does this happen?

HIV is a virus that can be transmitted from person to person through the sharing of certain bodily fluids: Blood, Semen and pre-seminal fluid, Vaginal fluids, Rectal fluids, and Breast milk.

How does it transmit?

  • Unprotected vaginal or anal sex
  • Sharing needles, syringes, or other drug injection equipment
  • Getting stuck with a needle that has the blood of someone with HIV on it
  • From mother to baby during pregnancy, childbirth, or breastfeeding

Preventive Measures:

1]Condoms:
Use latex condoms every time you have sex. Plastic or synthetic rubber condoms can be used if you have a latex allergy, but they break more easily than latex condoms. Natural membrane condoms, like lambskin, should not be used to prevent HIV.
2]Pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP):
PrEP is a prescription medication that can greatly reduce the risk of HIV. Side effects may include nausea, diarrhea, headache, fatigue, or stomach pain, but they are usually not serious and go away over time.
3]Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP):
PEP is a medication that can prevent HIV after a possible exposure. You must take PEP every day for 28 days, and you will need to return to your health care provider for follow-up testing.
4]Abstinence:
Abstaining from sex is one way to prevent HIV.
5]Avoid sharing needles:
Never share needles or other drug injection equipment.
6]Get tested:
Get tested for HIV every three to six months. You can buy a self-test kit at a drugstore or online.
7]Get tested and treated for STDs:
Having another STD can increase the chance of getting HIV.
8]Male circumcision:
In high-burden countries in eastern and southern Africa, male circumcision is recommended.
9]Use lube:
Applying lube can help minimize friction and reduce the risk of a condom breaking or sliding off.
